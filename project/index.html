About

What is ITFT ?

It is one of its kind in the Road Traffic Sector.
Intelligent Traffic Forecasting Tool (ITFT) is a very sophisticated and efficient traffic flow forecasting tool which uses combinations of a variety of traditional forecasting techniques under Machine Learning such as ARIMA, SARIMA etc.

Our AIM

ITFT aims at forecasting traffic flow at future time stamps at a given section of a road/highway. It uses a large amount of available past years time series flow data available for that section. The fairly forecasted data is extremely helpful in planning future capacity,comfort and geometric parameters of road, to analyze and eliminate potential environmental threats that may arise due to possible future congestion levels.

About

Features of ITFT

  • Fast ,Accurate and Reliable Forecasting.
  • No burden of manual analysis of given data to set up parameters for traffic forecasting models.
  • Not limited to a single forecasting method.
  • Capable of handling large amounts of real time data.
  • No need to do multiple refitting procedures as it was in case of traditional models.

Application

Utility of ITFT

Traffic volume forecasting

It is used for accurately forecasting the traffic volume using previous traffic volume trends and processing it with the help of ML to get the expected traffic situation in future.

Advanced Signalling Mechanism

It may be deployed as an advanced signalling mechanism which combines both ML techniques and live traffic flow for appropriate movement of traffic in various directions.

Avoiding Infrastructure Damage

It is helpful in analysing the demand for road infrastructure modification forced by the frequent traffic flow disruption. It may be solved by preparing alternative routes, widening of roads, etc.

Handling Heavy Traffic

It is helpful in handling heavy traffic flow at any busy intersection as we get to know the possible times at which traffic flow might slow down due to heavy volume of vehicles.

Limiting Pollution

It may help in reduction of bulk Co2 emission in a congested traffic. Stagnant or very slow traffic flow may lead to excessive pollution and emission of harmful other gases.
